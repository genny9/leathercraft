Projects Guide

The best thing to do when you're just beginning is to make something that interests you. Not only does this help keep you passionate about completing the project, it also helps you narrow your focus on a few leather working skills instead of being overwhelmed by them all at once. Pick something that is smaller and simple to make, so you can find out if you like leather working before you make a large investment. 

There are endless possibilities when it comes to leather crafting, and we have compiled several craft projects that you can try out. 

Keep reading to discover amazing leather crafts you can create in a breeze.

Leather Projects to try out today! 

Leather Belt

Making a leather belt is one of the easiest leather craft projects you can do. If you’re reading this now and wondering whether or not you have the ability to make one, rest assured that you can! It really is that easy. 

When you’ve finished, you will have a wonderful sense of accomplishment along with newfound knowledge to take your leather crafting skills to the next level. So, let’s get started.

What you need:
  • Leather 
  • Belt buckle
  • Measuring tape or ruler 
  • Long straight edge 
  • Utility or other sharp knife 
  • Rotary punch
  • Rivet setter 
  • Rivets 
  • Olive oil (or other finish of your choice)

Leather Slim Cardholder

This simple wallet is an easy project for someone who is first beginning leatherwork. If you’re new to the craft or this is your very first project, expect it to take about three hours to complete. Even though three hours isn’t too long, making this wallet will help you develop some important skills for leathering working like cutting, stitching, and edge finishing. You will also make a killer wallet to make your friends look with envy!

What you need:
  • Leather 
  • Cutting tool (Rotary Cutter / X-acto Knife / Round Knife)
  • Leather glue
  • Thread
  • Stitching Needles
  • Diamond Chisel
  • Stitching Groover
  • Burnisher or a Piece of Canvas
  • Edge Beveler
  • Gum Tragacanth
  • Dye and Carnauba Cream (if the leather isn't pre-dyed)

Leather Coin Purse

This small accessory is a great project to start on for your first few projects. T​ired of carrying a tonne of coins in your wallet or purse, or having them loose all around the place? We hear you!

This coin purse is a simple solution. The minimalist design and high quality leather makes it a stylish way to store loose change, with an easy to use button clasp closure.

What you need:
  • Leather 
  • Button stud
  • Leather coin purse template
  • Puncher
  • Diamond chisel
  • Hammer
  • Cutting tool
  • Sewing needle and thread
